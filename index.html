Gameplay Essentials

1) Team identification: Humans must wear their bandana around the wrist/arm and zombies must wear it around the head. It must be visible at all times!

2) Tags: A player joins the zombies when tagged by a zombie who is not stunned (see #3 and 4).

  • The tag counts if any part of the zombie touches any part of the human or anything they’re wearing or carrying, including blasters and clothing!
  • If you have any game items/weapons, drop them for your squad to recover (if they’re right next to you) or return it to a mod.
  • Note to taggers: no full-body contact. Tackling people to the ground is not safe, and not what players signed up for. Same goes for bear hugs, biting, inappropriate touching, etc - the only contact players are consenting to is being tagged.

3) Stunning zombies

  • A zombie becomes stunned (i.e. unable to tag or engage) when hit by a game-approved projectile (Nerf dart, sock, or smiley ball) or melee weapon (foam bat, etc) anywhere below the neck, unless they have a special ability that makes them invulnerable (see Special Zombies).
  • Sock and balls must be thrown and darts must be launched from a blaster for the stun to count.
  • To reiterate, headshots don’t count, so please do not aim for the head.
  • Once the projectile hits the ground, it can no longer stun a zombie. (Bounces off walls are okay!)

4) Stunned zombies

  • If stunned, a zombie pulls their bandana down to their neck, puts their hands up, and refrains from playing (tagging, chasing, scouting, etc.) until doing one of three things:
  • Touching a blue emergency phone tower
  • Take a 20-second breather 30 feet from the nearest human (about the length of 4 deep breaths)
  • Touching a special gravemind zombie and counting to 10

5) Be a good sport and have fun!! (most important rule)

The Nitty-Gritty: Additional Safety + Fairness Rules

  • No vehicles (bikes, skateboards, scooters, etc.) may be used during the game.
  • Non-players may not be used for tactical advantage (eg. as shields, to scout locations for you, etc). On a related note:
  • Joining mid-game: players are welcome join the game once they get a bandana from a moderator (eg. at Human HQ) and are absolutely clear on all the rules.
  • Bringing your own gear: while by no means required, you may bring your own sock grenades (no hard or explosive contents) and Nerf blasters. Modded blasters must be approved by the moderators in advance and fire darts no faster than 120 fps. Outside melee weapons and hard-tipped darts are not allowed.
  • Fenced-off construction areas and Main Quad are off-limits.
  • Restrooms, dining halls, restaurants, and academic buildings are no-combat zones. If you have both feet inside of any of these locations, you can’t be tagged or stunned—you’re safe!
  • For safety, staircases may only be used for transit (unless designated otherwise by a mod). Like the above, no tags or stuns, but unlike the above, no loitering on them to exploit this rule. (If someone tries to use it as a “safe zone” like one of the above indoor areas, they have 30 seconds to get off the stairs before they get automatically zombified/stunned.)
